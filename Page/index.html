Summary

As you can see, Toyota provided footnote disclosures that detail its operating and capital lease liabilities broken by time bands. Operating lease payments are not discounted. These payments represent the rental payments due in the years ahead. Capital lease payments are discounted and the present value of these payments represents the liability amount recognised in the balance sheet. Toyota's Disclosures of its Capital (Finance) Lease Commitments

GAAPs require lessees to recognise capital lease arrangements on the balance sheet as lease assets and lease liabilities. GAAPs also require lessees to disclose

  1. Information about the carrying amount of each class of lease assets at the reporting date
  2. a reconciliation of lease commitments to the carrying amount of lease liabilities broken down by time bands

The net book value of the lease assets for Toyota as of March 31st 2002 is US$481 million, made up of buildings, machinery and equipment.

Toyota's Disclosures of its Operating Lease Commitments

Unlike that of capital leases, GAAPs do not require lessees to recognise operating lease assets or liabilities on the balance sheet
However, GAAPs require the lessee to provide footnote disclosures about:

  1. the total amount of rental expense recognised in the financial year, with separate amounts for minimum lease payments
  2. non-cancellable operating leases, the minimum lease payments and, where different from the minimum lease payments, the lease commitments broken down by time bands, according to the time that is expected to elapse from the reporting date to the expected date of payment

Toyota Corp.
Finance (or Capital) Lease Disclosures

Toyota's footnote disclosures show that its rental expense associated with operating leases amounted to $548 million during 2002. This figure also appears in Toyota's income statement as a lease rental expense.
Toyota also provided separate disclosures about its (undiscounted) minimum rental payments associated with its operating leases for each of the next five years and for the combined period beyond five years.

Rental expense under operating leases for the years ending March 31st 2000, 2001 and 2002 were ¥ 74,500 million, ¥ 64,744 million and ¥ 72,989 million ($548 million) respectively.
